2. Description of Service

Pluma is a HIPAA-compliant intake application that collects self-reported environmental, occupational, and symptom data associated with Parkinson's disease. The Service packages responses into structured records for downstream clinical or research analysis alongside multi-omics data.

The Service does not diagnose any condition, compute a risk score, or provide medical advice. It is not a substitute for evaluation by a qualified clinician.

3. HIPAA Compliance

Pluma is designed and operated in accordance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA) and its implementing regulations, including the Privacy Rule (45 CFR Part 160 and Subparts A and E of Part 164) and the Security Rule (45 CFR Part 160 and Subparts A and C of Part 164).

  • Protected Health Information (PHI) — all intake responses, ZIP codes, and associated metadata are treated as PHI. These fields are encrypted at rest using AES-256-GCM and are accessible only to authorized users through role-based access controls.
  • Minimum Necessary Standard — researchers may access only the records of patients who have explicitly granted them permission. The root administrator has full access for system administration purposes as permitted under HIPAA's administrative provisions.
  • Audit Controls (§ 164.312(b)) — every access to, modification of, or deletion of PHI is recorded in an immutable audit log that captures the actor, timestamp, IP address, user agent, target resource, and outcome.
  • Access Controls (§ 164.312(a)) — the Service enforces unique user identification, automatic session expiration, multi-factor authentication (TOTP and WebAuthn/passkeys), and emergency access procedures.
  • Transmission Security (§ 164.312(e)) — all data in transit is protected via TLS. The Service enforces HTTPS in production with HSTS preloading and strict Content Security Policy headers.
  • Business Associate Agreements — if you are a covered entity or business associate using this Service, a BAA is required before any PHI is processed. Contact the Service operator to execute a BAA.
