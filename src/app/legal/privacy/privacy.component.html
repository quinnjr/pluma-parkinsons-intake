Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 19, 2026

1. Overview

This Privacy Policy describes how Pluma ("the Service") collects, uses, stores, and protects your information. The Service is designed to comply with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA), including the Privacy Rule and the Security Rule. We are committed to safeguarding your Protected Health Information (PHI).

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Account Information

  • Email address — used for account identification, login, email verification, and password-reset communications.
  • Password — stored only as an argon2id hash. We never store or transmit your plaintext password.
  • Authentication credentials — TOTP secrets (encrypted at rest), recovery passcode hashes, and WebAuthn public keys for passkey-based authentication.

2.2 Intake Data (Protected Health Information)

When you complete the intake form, we collect self-reported data including:

  • Demographic information (age band, sex at birth)
  • Environmental and occupational exposure history
  • Medical and lifestyle history
  • Motor and non-motor symptom assessments
  • Free-text narrative responses
  • ZIP code (optional; used for geo-correlation with environmental registries)

Direct identifiers (name, email address) are stripped client-side before your intake data is transmitted to the server. The server rejects any submission that contains direct PII keys.

2.3 Audit and Security Data

  • IP address and user agent for each authenticated request
  • Timestamps and outcomes of login attempts, data access events, and administrative actions
  • These records are required by HIPAA § 164.312(b) for audit control purposes

3. How We Protect Your Data

3.1 Encryption at Rest

All PHI fields — including your intake responses, ZIP code, and TOTP authentication secrets — are encrypted using AES-256-GCM with a key derived via scrypt from a server-side secret. Only ciphertext is stored in the database. Decryption occurs only at the point of authorized access.

3.2 Encryption in Transit

All communications between your browser and the Service are encrypted via TLS. The Service enforces HTTPS in production with HTTP Strict Transport Security (HSTS) preloading and a two-year max-age directive.

3.3 Access Controls

  • Role-based access control (RBAC) — three distinct roles (patient, researcher, root administrator) with enforced separation of privileges.
  • Patient-controlled researcher access — researchers can view your records only after you explicitly grant permission. You may revoke access at any time, effective immediately.
  • Multi-factor authentication — optional TOTP-based second factor with single-use recovery passcodes, and WebAuthn passkey support (Touch ID, Windows Hello, security keys).
  • Automatic session expiration — sessions expire after 8 hours (configurable by the Service operator).
  • Account lockout — 5 failed login attempts within 15 minutes triggers a temporary lockout for that account.

3.4 Audit Logging

Every access to, modification of, or deletion of PHI generates an immutable audit log entry. These logs record the actor, timestamp, IP address, user agent, target resource, action performed, and outcome. Audit logs are accessible only to the root administrator and are retained in accordance with HIPAA's six-year retention requirement.

4. How We Use Your Data

  • Intake records are stored encrypted for your access and, with your explicit consent (via researcher grants), for authorized researcher review. Records are packaged as LLM-ready markdown for downstream analysis alongside multi-omics data.
  • Email address is used solely for account authentication, email verification, and password reset. We do not send marketing communications.
  • Audit data is used for security monitoring, incident investigation, and HIPAA compliance. It is never used for profiling or marketing.

5. Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information or PHI. Your data is shared only in the following circumstances:

  • With researchers you authorize — only after you grant explicit access through the Service's grant mechanism.
  • With the root administrator — for system administration purposes as required by HIPAA's administrative provisions.
  • As required by law — in response to a valid subpoena, court order, or government investigation, in compliance with HIPAA's permitted disclosures.
  • Business associates — service providers who process data on our behalf are bound by Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) as required by HIPAA.

6. Your Rights

Under HIPAA and applicable state law, you have the right to:

  • Access — view and download your intake records at any time through your patient dashboard.
  • Amendment — edit your submitted intake records through the Service interface.
  • Accounting of disclosures — request an accounting of who has accessed your records via the audit log (contact the root administrator).
  • Restriction — grant or revoke researcher access to your records at your sole discretion, effective immediately.
  • Deletion — delete individual records or your entire account, which permanently and irreversibly removes all associated data including intake records, authentication credentials, and access grants.
  • Breach notification — receive notification in the event of a breach of your unsecured PHI, as required by the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule.

7. Data Retention

  • Intake records are retained until you delete them or delete your account.
  • Audit logs are retained for a minimum of six (6) years in compliance with HIPAA's documentation retention requirements (45 CFR § 164.530(j)).
  • Account data is retained until you delete your account. Upon account deletion, all associated records, tokens, grants, and credentials are permanently removed via cascading deletion.
  • Password reset and verification tokens — only sha256 hashes are stored; they expire automatically (1 hour for reset tokens, 30 minutes for verification codes) and are marked as used upon redemption.

8. Cookies and Tracking

The Service uses a single HTTP-only, SameSite=Strict session cookie for authentication. This cookie contains a signed JWT and cannot be read by client-side JavaScript. We do not use tracking cookies, analytics pixels, or third-party advertising scripts.

9. Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed at individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect information from minors. If you believe a minor has provided information through the Service, contact the Service operator for removal.

10. International Users

The Service is operated in the State of Florida, United States, and is subject to U.S. federal law (including HIPAA) and the laws of the State of Florida. If you access the Service from outside the United States, you consent to the transfer and processing of your data in the United States.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. Material changes will be posted on this page with an updated effective date. Continued use of the Service constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

12. Contact

For questions about this Privacy Policy, to exercise your HIPAA rights, to request an accounting of disclosures, or to report a security concern, contact the Service operator's designated Privacy Officer at the contact information provided during account registration or system deployment.
